Air Quality Around the World

By James Dunstan

Air quality is important to our health. When we breathe in oxygen, if there are any pollutants in the air, it can enter out bloodstream and cause various health issues. Below is a map of the world showcasing each country's Air Quality Index (AQI), find out how healthy your country's air is!

AQI Map

The AQI Map shows the AQI category as well as the AQI value of a country and its representing cities. The AQI values are categorised from Good (0-50), Moderate (51-100), Unhealthy (101-200), Very Unhealthy (200-300) and Hazardous (300+). The bigger the points on the map, the bigger the AQI value.

Hover over a point to find out information about it! If you want to only look at specific categories or countries, then select them through the filters! The time graph will also change with the filters.

Figure 1: AQI World Map showcasing the world's AQI category and values


Ozone Health


The Ozone Layer can be affected by poor air quality as pollutents such as NO2 can cause holes in said layer. In turn, as the ozone layer weakens, more ultraviolet radiation can pour in and thus cause more health issues. Below are two pie charts show casing the different Ozone and NO2 AQI values across the world.

CO Health


An increase in CO gas in an area can lead to not only environmental damage, but also to human health as we inhale larger quanties that that is safe. Below is a bar chart that details the CO AQI Values across the world.

CO AQI Bar Chart

To the right is a bar chart displaying the CO AQI values around the world. The higher the bar, the higher or more combined CO values there are for a given country.

You can drag the slider to alter the bar chart to show all values ABOVE the selected range. To see only specific countries, use the drop down menu to select them.

How Air Quality Affects Water Quality

Air quality can have impacts on the water quality. When large amounts of air pollutants enter bodies of water, they contaminate it and cause health issues when consumed or used in argriculture. Below is a scatterplot showing the relationship between water and air quality of a country.

Water vs Air Quality Scatterplot

To the right is a scatterplot of air vs water quality of a given country and its cities. Higher number on either axis means more pollution on either their air, water or both quantities.

You can use the drop down menu to filter to specific counties
